# Web Scraping and Data Collection

This repository accompanies a **three-day academic workshop** on web scraping and data collection designed for **early-career academic professionals** and **advanced doctoral students** in **Health Sciences, Life Sciences, and the Social Sciences**.

The workshop is conceived as a **no-code research methods seminar**. Participants are not expected to code. Instead, they work through a guided workflow supported by scripts in **Python** and **R** and an accessible interface through which they can upload, process, inspect, and export data. The central aim is to show how web-based information can be transformed into structured research material in a practical, methodologically coherent, and accessible way.

The workshop focuses on how researchers can collect information from online sources, organize and clean the resulting outputs, and conduct an initial exploration of the data within a no-code environment. Its disciplinary orientation toward the health, life, and social sciences makes the applications concrete and relevant, including examples such as clinical trial registries, institutional research pages, funding calls, public directories, legislative records, policy materials, and organizational websites.

Pedagogically, the workshop progresses from **source identification**, to **data preparation**, to **participant-specific adaptation**. Across the three days, participants learn how structured information can be collected from websites, how raw outputs can be cleaned and standardized, and how the workflow can be adapted to their own URLs, spreadsheets, or existing datasets. The workshop also includes an explicit discussion of the **ethics of web scraping and digital data collection**, emphasizing responsible use, transparency, and research judgment.
